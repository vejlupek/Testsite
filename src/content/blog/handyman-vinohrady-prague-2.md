Prague 2 – Vinohrady and Nusle are among the most densely populated districts in Prague. Turn-of-the-century apartment buildings, elegant inter-war houses, and modern refurbished flats. I work here every week, and I know exactly what problems come up most often.

## What I most commonly fix in Vinohrady flats

### Old doors, locks and hardware

Vinohrady buildings have character – and their quirks. Old door frames, locks that jam, handles that have worked loose – I see these in every other flat. I replace cylinder locks, adjust doors, or fit new security hardware.

### Painting and plaster repairs

Older flats develop cracks over time, wallpaper starts peeling, or the walls just look tired. Painting a single room takes me half a day. I prepare the surface, fill any cracks, and leave it smooth and clean.

### Furniture assembly

Vinohrady has a high turnover of tenants – people move in and out every month. Assembling IKEA, JYSK or other furniture before moving in, or disassembling it before moving out – I do this regularly. One PAX wardrobe takes me 45–60 minutes; a whole room in an afternoon.

### Drilling into historic walls

Vinohrady buildings are usually brick or concrete – depending on the era and the specific wall. The right wall plug and technique are crucial; without experience it often goes wrong. I drill precisely, with no unnecessary extra holes.

## Nusle – Jižní svah, Táborská

I visit Nusle just as regularly as Vinohrady. Older housing stock, panel blocks near Táborská, and refurbished flats on the Jižní svah. Typical requests: floor repairs, toilet cistern replacement, shelf assembly, or hanging pictures and mirrors.
